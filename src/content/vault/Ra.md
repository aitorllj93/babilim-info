## Introduction

Ra (also known as Re) was the supreme solar deity of ancient Egyptian religion, representing the primary force of creation and life itself. As the sun god par excellence, he was considered the king of the gods and the patron of Egyptian pharaohs, who claimed divine descent from him <mcreference link="https://www.worldhistory.org/Ra_(Egyptian_God)/" index="1">1</mcreference> <mcreference link="https://www.britannica.com/topic/Re" index="2">2</mcreference>.

## Myths and Legends

**The Daily Solar Journey**  
Ra's most fundamental myth describes his daily journey across the sky in his solar bark. Each day, he would emerge from the eastern horizon, traveling across the heavens in his day-bark called Mandjet. At sunset, he would begin his perilous night journey through the underworld in his night-bark called Mesektet, fighting the serpent [[Apophis]] who sought to destroy him and prevent the sun's return <mcreference link="https://www.worldhistory.org/Ra_(Egyptian_God)/" index="1">1</mcreference>.

**Creation of the World**  
As a creator god, Ra emerged from the primordial waters of [[Nun]], standing upon the first mound of creation. He then produced the first gods through his creative powers, either through spitting or through his divine utterance <mcreference link="https://www.britannica.com/topic/Re" index="2">2</mcreference>.

**The Secret Name of Ra**  
One of the most famous myths involves [[Isis]] who sought to gain power over Ra by learning his secret name. She created a serpent to poison him and only offered the cure if he revealed his true name, thus gaining power over him <mcreference link="https://www.worldhistory.org/article/885/the-secret-name-of-ra/" index="3">3</mcreference>.
