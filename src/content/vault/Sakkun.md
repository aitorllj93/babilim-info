##  Introduction
Sakkun was a significant Phoenician deity whose worship spanned several centuries, known primarily through theophoric names and sacred inscriptions. His influence in Phoenician religion is evidenced by his incorporation into important personal names and his presence in religious texts.

---

## Etymology

- **Root meaning**: The name Sakkun (ê§éê§äê§ç skn) likely relates to the concept of "administration" or "superintendence"
- **Language of origin**: Phoenician
- **Variants and evolution**: 
  - Also appears as Sakun, Sakkon, or Sakon in various transliterations
  - Found in compound names like Sanchuniathon ("Sakkun has given")
- **Symbolic implications**: Associated with divine administration and oversight

##  Cultural Context

- **Civilization**: Phoenician

- **Historical Period**: At least from 5th century BCE onwards

## Myths and Legends

**Divine Administration**  
While specific myths about Sakkun are scarce, his importance is reflected in the theophoric names that incorporate his divine presence, suggesting his role in bestowing divine favor and oversight.

**Sacred Knowledge**  
The connection of Sakkun to sacred writings and temple inscriptions, as evidenced through archaeological findings, suggests a role in divine wisdom and administration of sacred knowledge.
